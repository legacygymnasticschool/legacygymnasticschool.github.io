Legacy School of Gymnastics Classes

Amethysts: ages 3-4

45 min class
$75 once per week
$140 twice per week

This fun class for the young ones is designed to learn basic movements and skills of Rhythmic Gymnastics including balances, turns and jumps. Playing with some of Rhythmic apparatuses (rope, hoop, ribbon and ball) will help improve hand-eye coordination while simple stretching exercises provide foundation for developing flexibility, posture and beautiful body lines. We will begin introducing basic acrobatic skills (rolls and cartwheels). They will also learn the basics of ballet and are encouraged to explore their expression in dance.

Emeralds: ages 5-6

55 min class
$80 once per week
$150 twice per week

This exciting and active class is designed to introduce girls to Rhythmic Gymnastics. This class includes simple stretching exercises, jumping rope, balancing and acrobatics along with basic movements of Rhythmic Gymnastics, including hand-eye coordination with ball, hoop, rope and ribbon. The main focus of this class is establishing correct body and apparatus techniques, providing the foundation for growth, and with the potential advancement to the competitive program. Students will develop flexibility, coordination, balance and strength.

Sapphires: ages 6+

55 min class
$80 once per week
$150 twice per week

This beginning level class is designed to introduce girls to the sport of Rhythmic Gymnastics. This class focuses on basic body elements (flexibility, balance, jumps & turns), acrobatics (rolls, cartwheels, bridges) and apparatus techniques (rope, hoop, ball, clubs and ribbon). The main focus of this class is to establish correct body and apparatus techniques, while providing the foundation for growth, and potential advancement to the competitive program. Students will develop flexibility, coordination, balance and strength.

Pre-team/Rubies: ages 5+

1.5 hours

This class is by invitation only. It is designed to prepare gymnasts for the competitive team in the following season. They will perfect their basic body movements, acrobatics, and apparatus techniques with the rope, hoop, and ball. This excellent conditioning program will not only make girls fit but will also prevent injury while allowing them to learn cool and amazing Rhythmic skills safely. Stretching techniques and exercises include over splits and extensive back stretching. They will begin to learn about the dedication, perseverance, and hard work it takes to compete.

Diamonds: ages 6+

1.5 hours

This intermediate class is designed for higher level gymnasts or advanced dancers that are looking for an alternative to the competitive team. Students learn body and apparatus skills that help build a strong and flexible body for gymnasts, dancers, cheerleaders and figure skaters. This excellent conditioning program will not only make girls fit but will prevent injury allowing them to learn cool and amazing Rhythmic skills safely. Stretching techniques and exercises include over splits and extensive back stretching. Girls will also learn Rhythmic skills (turns, balances, leaps and acrobatics). Practicing with Rhythmic apparatuses is not only fun, but helps develop coordination and agility.

Competition Team: ages 6+

Competitive team classes offered by invitation only.

Our competitive programs requires some dedication, but the results are extremely rewarding. Not only is it fun and challenging to learn difficult and interesting skills (body and apparatus), it’s also fun to compete and see the results of all the hard work. The competitive team emphasizes good sportsmanship, responsibility, determination, commitment, goal setting and more. In addition to all these important life lessons, it’s just fun to wear pretty colorful leotards and show everyone what you can do!

XCEL: ages 6+

1.5 hours- 3 hours per week

The Rhythmic XCEL program allows kids to have positive competitive experiences in an official USA Gymnastics setting with less commitment and training time required. The program also allows athletes to participate in Rhythmic Gymnastics while staying active in school and family activities. The rules for Rhythmic Xcel are designed to put the focus on personal progress rather than competitive placement. Therefore, athletes can be challenged and happy in a single level for multiple years. The gymnasts competing in XCEL will not be ranked by score, they only compete against themselves. The XCEL competition season is shorter than a regular competition season and gymnasts are only required to commit to half of the year, and may choose to take the other half of the year off.

Junior Olympic and FIG Program: ages 6+

Level 3: 5 hours per week
Level 4: 7.5 hours per week
Level 5: 12 hours per week
Level 6: 16 hours per week
Level 7-8: 16-20 hours per week

The Rhythmic Junior Olympic Program is a developmental program that helps prepare athletes from the beginning stages of Rhythmic until the time they are ready to make the move to the Elite Program (FIG). Our Junior Olympic (Levels 3-8) and FIG (Levels 9 & 10) team programs are governed by the USA Gymnastics organization and are developed and judged based on the International Code of Points. These intense, competitive programs are preparing gymnasts for state, regional, national and international championships and competitions. The gymnasts begin training in July, which includes required attendance to learn choreographed routines in preparation for competitions that begin in December and continue through the following June.

Private Lessons

Private lessons are strongly encouraged for gymnasts at the higher levels of programs. One-on-one time with a competitive team coach will help give gymnasts a competitive edge and act as a catalyst for performance breakthroughs. We also offer private lessons for dancers, cheerleaders, and ice skaters who want to increase their flexibility and strength to improve their performance. Private lessons are scheduled directly through the owner.
